# ANGLES — Conversion Hooks Library

## Core Rule

An angle is the difference between:
- ignored content
- clicked content

Same product, different angle = different results.

Rule:
- do not lead with the product
- lead with the problem, benefit, comparison, or curiosity

## 1. Buyer Intent Angles (highest priority)

Use when goal = clicks + conversions.

- Best X for Y
  - "Best budget desk setup for small spaces"
- Top X under $X
  - "Top 5 gadgets under $25 that actually work"
- X that actually works
  - "Skin care that actually works for oily skin"
- Worth it?
  - "Is this $20 kitchen tool actually worth it?"
- Stop buying X, get this instead
- Upgrade your X with this
- Things you did not know you needed for X

Best for:
- Amazon
- Pinterest
- product roundups
- direct-response affiliate posts

## 2. Problem -> Solution Angles

Use when pain is obvious and immediate.

- Fix this problem fast
  - "Fix your messy desk in 10 minutes"
- If you struggle with X
- Tired of X?
- Avoid this mistake
- You are doing X wrong
- This solves X instantly

These convert well because they map directly to pain.

Best for:
- buyer-intent keywords
- practical products
- "before the click, the user already knows the problem"

## 3. Curiosity / Scroll-Stopping Angles

Use to increase CTR, not as a substitute for usefulness.

- Nobody talks about this
- I did not expect this to work
- This feels illegal but works
- I tried this so you do not have to
- This changed everything for me
- You will not believe this works

Rule:
- curiosity must still resolve into a clear benefit
- do not overclickbait without payoff

Best for:
- social posts
- trend tests
- "viral stuff that works" type lanes

## 4. List / Collection Angles (Pinterest gold)

- X ideas you will actually use
- X things that make life easier
- X essentials for beginners
- X things you need in your life
- X setup ideas
- X aesthetic but useful ideas

These are highly repeatable and easy to scale.

Best for:
- Pinterest batches
- seasonal gift lists
- evergreen product collections

## 5. Identity Angles (support layer)

Use for softer posts and audience bonding.

- If you are the kind of person who...
- For people who love X
- Things only X people understand
- This is your sign to start X

Not primary money drivers, but useful for engagement and audience fit.

Rule:
- identity alone is not enough
- pair it with usefulness, giftability, or curiosity

## 6. Comparison Angles

- X vs Y (which is better?)
- Cheap vs expensive version
- Amazon vs DIY
- This vs that

Great for affiliate links because they create decision pressure.

Best for:
- products with obvious substitutes
- budget buyers
- "worth it?" decision posts

## 7. Seasonal Angles

- Fall essentials for X
- Christmas gift ideas for X
- Back-to-school X
- Summer setup ideas
- Halloween picks for X

Use to spike traffic during predictable buying windows.

Rule:
- seasonal content must launch early enough to matter
- do not publish expired seasonal content into the active queue

## 8. Transformation Angles

- Before / after
- Messy -> clean
- Basic -> upgraded
- Cheap -> aesthetic
- Empty -> finished

Highly visual and usually strong on Pinterest.

Best for:
- room changes
- beauty
- organization
- setup improvements

## 9. Speed / Ease Angles

- In 5 minutes
- No effort needed
- Beginner-friendly
- Zero experience required
- Lazy version of X

Lower friction = higher clicks.

Best for:
- beginners
- low-energy buyers
- fast-win products

## 10. Price Anchoring Angles

- Under $10
- Cheap but good
- Budget-friendly X
- Looks expensive but is not
- Under $25 and actually useful

These convert hard on Amazon because they reduce buyer hesitation.

Best for:
- impulse-buy ranges
- roundup posts
- giftable finds

## 11. Save / Copy Angles

- Steal this setup
- Copy this idea
- Save this for later
- Do not lose this

Good for saves, which can help Pinterest distribution.

Best for:
- setup posts
- checklists
- visual lists

## 12. Combo Angles (important)

Most winning content combines 2-3 angles.

Examples:
- "5 budget desk setup ideas that actually work"
- "Fix your messy desk with these cheap Amazon finds"
- "Beginner-friendly plants that are hard to kill"

Do not rely on single-angle content unless the hook is unusually strong.

Safe combos:
- List + Budget + Useful
- Problem + Fast + Cheap
- Curiosity + Worth It + Visual Proof
- Seasonal + Gift + Under $25
- Identity + Useful + Save This

## 13. Angle Filters

Reject angles that:
- do not imply action
- do not imply benefit
- are purely aesthetic
- need too much explanation before the click

Accept angles that:
- imply improvement
- imply curiosity
- imply usefulness
- imply a decision the buyer already wants to make

## 14. Default Angle Stacks

If unsure, use one of these:

- List + Budget + Useful
  - "10 cheap Amazon finds that make your life easier"
- Problem + Solution + Fast
  - "Fix this annoying kitchen problem in 5 minutes"
- Worth It + Comparison
  - "Is this viral product actually worth it?"
- Seasonal + Gift + Clickable
  - "Cute Halloween picks under $25"

## 15. Operational Rule

When selecting an angle:
- choose the angle first
- then attach the product
- then confirm the visual

Do not reverse this by forcing a weak angle onto a product just because the product exists.
